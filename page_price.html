Frequently Asked Questions

How can I get a 5G connection on my iPhone 12?
All iPhone 12 and iPhone 12 Pro models are 5G capable. For 5G access, you need to activate your iPhone with a carrier that
offers 5G service. Some carriers may also require a 5G service plan or a new SIM. Contact your carrier for more
information. To learn more, visit https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT204039
Which U.S. wireless carriers offer 5G service?
AT&T, Sprint, T-Mobile, and Verizon are the national wireless carriers for iPhone in the United States, and they all offer 5G
service. Apple.com and the Apple Store sell iPhone models for use on these networks.

What does “SIM-free” mean?
“SIM-free” means your iPhone doesn’t ship with a carrier nano-SIM. The SIM-free iPhone sold on apple.com and at the
Apple Store is unlocked. That means you can use a nano-SIM or eSIM from any carrier that provides service for iPhone.*

* Use of eSIM requires a wireless service plan (which may include restrictions on switching service providers and roaming,
even after contract expiration). Not all carriers support eSIM. Use of eSIM in iPhone may be disabled when purchased from
some carriers. See your carrier for details. To learn more, visit support.apple.com/en-us/HT209044.

What is eSIM?
eSIM technology allows you to add a cellular plan to your iPhone without using a nano-SIM card. Activating an eSIM will
require scanning a valid QR code distributed by a wireless carrier, or signing up for a participating plan through a wireless
carrier app.*

* Use of eSIM requires a wireless service plan (which may include restrictions on switching service providers and roaming,
even after contract expiration). Not all carriers support eSIM. Use of eSIM in iPhone may be disabled when purchased from
some carriers. See your carrier for details. To learn more, visit support.apple.com/en-us/HT209044.

When I buy an iPhone from Apple, will the eSIM be activated?
Carrier-connected models of iPhone purchased from Apple come with a physical nano-SIM, which will be used to activate
your wireless account. To activate the eSIM on your new iPhone, please contact your carrier once you receive it.*

* Use of eSIM requires a wireless service plan (which may include restrictions on switching service providers and roaming,
even after contract expiration). Not all carriers support eSIM. Use of eSIM in iPhone may be disabled when purchased from
some carriers. See your carrier for details. To learn more, visit support.apple.com/en-us/HT209044.

Are there differences between an iPhone for AT&T, T-Mobile, Sprint, or Verizon?

The iPhone models for AT&T, T-Mobile, Sprint, and Verizon are the same. If you choose to pay with an AT&T Installment
plan, your iPhone will be locked to the carrier. For details on network support, please see https://support.apple.com/en-
us/HT204039.

When I buy from apple.com, does my iPhone come ready to use?
If you completed the steps to authorize activation with AT&T, Sprint, T-Mobile, or Verizon when you purchased your new
iPhone online, it will arrive ready to use. Just turn it on and follow the onscreen instructions to set it up and activate with
the carrier. If you chose to skip carrier authorization at the time of purchase, you’ll need to contact your carrier or visit an
Apple Store to enable cellular services.

You can also set up your iPhone without a nano-SIM using Wi-Fi. You will need an active nano-SIM to enable cellular
services. If you selected the SIM-free iPhone, you’ll need to insert your own nano-SIM card or obtain one from the
supported carrier of your choice and activate it in order to use cellular services on your iPhone. If Sprint is your carrier,
you’ll need to get a new nano-SIM card in order to activate your iPhone 12 or iPhone 12 Pro.

Will my new iPhone be unlocked?
All iPhone models sold on apple.com and at the Apple Store are unlocked, which means they aren’t tied to a single carrier,
and you can use a SIM from the carrier of your choosing. The exception is when you buy an iPhone with an AT&T
Installment Plan. It will be locked to AT&T as the carrier and will only work on the AT&T network for the term of your
Installment Plan agreement.
What purchase options are there for buying an iPhone?

You can either pay for your new iPhone in full or pay monthly with Apple Card Monthly Installments, carrier financing, Apple
iPhone Payments, or the iPhone Upgrade Program. If you choose an Apple monthly payment option, your carrier service
plan will be charged separately. Just choose the option that works for you.
